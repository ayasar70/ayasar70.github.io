Research Experience :
  • Single PC Graph Management SystemSupervised by Prof. Ling Liu
    Georgia Institute of Technology 

    Although several disk based graph management systems have been proposed still they are limited and have several problems. In this project we study on efficient graph computations on a single PC. Our goal is to develop general purpose graph processing framework and software system in storage, access and computation tiers. Our approach leverages locality and sparseness of real graphs and proposes a novel parallel execution model for extended(HDDs and SSDs) and semi extended(SSD arrays) memory models.

  • Distributed Graph Management SystemSupervised by Prof. Ling Liu
    Georgia Institute of Technology 

    Communication is one of the most important problems for distributed graph management systems. In this project we study on efficient graph computations on distributed environments. Our goal is to develop general purpose dynamically distributed graph processing framework and software system in storage, access and computation tiers. Our approach runs on top of our single PC graph management system therefore it isn’t limited by distributed memory size and it also leverages from the locality aware processing.

  • M2M TrustSupervised by Prof. Ling Liu
    Georgia Institute of Technology 

    The project is developing algorithms and tools and open source systems for resilient trust management in machine to machine networks. My contribution to this project is to develop a scalable M2M interaction and trust relation network simulator.
  • Scalable layout of large graphs on diskSupervised by Assoc. Prof. Buğra Gedik
    Bilkent University 

    In this project we propose a novel scalable MapReduce style method (ICBL) for disk layout of graphs. ICBL consists of four major steps: (1) Identifying diffusion paths, (2) Coarse Partitioning, (3) Block formation, (4) Labeling. Since we propose to develop a scalable system, whole system is implemented on Hadoop.

  • Rh++Supervised by Dr. Sultan Turhan
    University of Galatasaray 

    Objective of Rh++ was to develop a social responsibility project whose goal is to accelarate and optimize pre-transfusion procedures of Turkish hospitals. Rh++ aims to find and redirect donors to centers quickly in need. For this we built a distributed system and necessary applications for users, donors and centers.

  • Random Number Generator on FPGAsSupervised by Samy Meftali
    INRIA Lille-Nord Europe 

    My objective for this project was to develop a dynamic and parallel random number generator on a ’Spartan 3E FPGA Family’. Behind the implementation part for proving the performance of that system I made system analysis and Monte Carlo tests for both serial and parallel versions.
